Frequently Asked Questions About Prenuptial and Postnuptial Agreements

How much does it cost to draft a prenuptial or postnuptial agreement with Aronov Family Law NY, P.C. in Queens, NY?
Our fees for prenuptial and postnuptial agreements typically start at $1,500, depending on the complexity of the agreement and the individual circumstances involved. We offer an initial consultation to discuss your needs and provide a more accurate estimate. Please note that if negotiations become more complex or require multiple revisions, additional hourly fees may apply. We’ll always provide a clear fee structure before you retain our services.
How long does it take to finalize a prenuptial or postnuptial agreement in New York?
The timeline can vary, but most prenuptial agreements that we draft are completed within 2 to 4 weeks, provided that both parties are responsive and there are no significant disagreements. Postnuptial agreements can take a similar amount of time. If negotiations are more involved, or if either party wishes to have another attorney review the agreement, the process may take longer. We recommend starting the process well in advance of your wedding or any major financial changes.
Is Aronov Family Law NY, P.C. licensed to provide legal services for prenuptial and postnuptial agreements in New York?
Yes, our attorneys are fully licensed to practice law in the state of New York and have extensive experience in family law, including prenuptial and postnuptial agreements. We stay up to date with New York’s specific requirements to ensure your agreement is enforceable and legally sound.
What is the process for creating a prenuptial or postnuptial agreement with your firm?
We begin with an initial consultation, either in-person at our Queens office or virtually, to understand your goals and financial situation. We then gather relevant financial information and discuss your preferences for asset division and other terms. Our attorneys draft a tailored agreement, review it with you, and make any necessary revisions. Both parties must have the opportunity for independent legal counsel, and both signatures must be notarized for the agreement to be valid under New York law. We guide you through every step to ensure all requirements are met.
Can you guarantee that my prenuptial or postnuptial agreement will be upheld in a New York court?
While we take every precaution to draft agreements that comply with New York law and maximize enforceability, no attorney can guarantee that a court will uphold every term of a prenuptial or postnuptial agreement. Courts can set aside provisions that are unconscionable or improperly executed. We ensure full financial disclosure and recommend that each party has independent legal representation, which greatly increases the likelihood your agreement will be enforced.
