Frequently Asked Questions About Property Division

How is property typically divided during a divorce in Queens, NY, and how can Aronov Family Law NY, P.C. help?
In Queens and throughout New York, property division follows the principle of 'equitable distribution.' This means marital assets are divided fairly, but not always equally. At Aronov Family Law NY, P.C., we work closely with clients to identify and value all marital assets—including homes, retirement accounts, businesses, and personal property—so we can advocate for a fair division based on your unique situation. We also handle complex asset cases and help ensure your rights are protected throughout the process.

How long does the property division process usually take in a New York divorce?
The timeline for property division in New York divorces can vary widely. For uncontested cases with minimal assets, the process can take a few months. However, if there are disputes over asset valuation or ownership, or if the case goes to court, it may take a year or longer. At Aronov Family Law NY, P.C., we strive to resolve cases as efficiently as possible while ensuring your interests are fully represented.
